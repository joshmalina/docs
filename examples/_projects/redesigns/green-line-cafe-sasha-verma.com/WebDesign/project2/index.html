Screens ‘N’ Spokes

This ain't your average charity gig. This October and November, Screens ‘N’ Spokes will celebrate art, bikes, and the community spirit they inspire. Green Line Cafe will feature a display of original, limited-run screen prints from nationally acclaimed rock poster artists. All artwork was donated exclusively for this benefit show.

Screens ‘N’ Spokes returns to Philadelphia for the fifth straight year. Since 2007, the Screens ‘N’ Spokes bike team has raised over $230,000 to benefit the Greater Delaware Valley Chapter of the National Multiple Sclerosis Society (NMSS). Screens ‘N’ Spokes was conceived by Sam Verrill. Sam's girlfriend, a Narberth local, was diagnosed with Multiple Sclerosis at age 20, during their junior year of college seven years ago.

Sam, an avid concertgoer and rock poster collector as well as a bike enthusiast, wanted a new way to contribute to the Bike MS City to Shore Ride. “The poster medium is a tried and true means of expressive communication, and we are trying with this show to publicize an extremely worthy and important cause,” Verrill said. “The idea was to really energize our peer group to take action and have fun with a charity.”

Each design is available as a framed or unframed print. They will be for sale online starting October 10 at www.screensnspokes.etsy.com. All proceeds will benefit the Bike MS City to Shore Bike Ride, a signature event of the Greater Delaware Valley Chapter of the NMSS.
October 25, 2011
